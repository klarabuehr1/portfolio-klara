SALT.

Workshop Mechatronics Arduino Laser Cutting

OVERVIEW

Salt was created during a one-week mechatronics workshop. The task was to recreate a simple everyday action and give the prototype a sense of character.
We chose to mechatronically recreate grinding salt with a salt mill, focusing on imitating the human grip and twisting motion.

PROCESS

01 Sketches

We used quick sketches to explore different ideas and understand how the twisting motion of a salt grinder could be translated into a mechatronic movement.

02 First Prototypes

The prototypes helped us test proportions, spatial arrangement, and the first gripping mechanism. It made early weaknesses visible and helped us refine the setup.

03 Implementation

During implementation, we tested motors, mechanical solutions, and gripping methods to develop the concept step by step into a functional prototype.

FINAL OUTCOME

The final prototype imitates the human gesture of using a salt grinder. Instead of rotating continuously and perfectly, the movement is slightly irregular, with small pauses and changing angles to make the interaction feel more human.

An Arduino controls the motor, which is triggered by a button and performs a random number of rotations. This makes the amount of salt feel slightly unpredictable, similar to being served by another person.

In the exhibition, the prototype was staged as a small dining situation. Visitors could press the button and watch the grinder add salt to the plate, turning a simple everyday action into a playful mechatronic interaction.
