DRYVE

Interface Usability User Interviews Prototyping Arduino

OVERVIEW

Dryve is an experimental controller designed to operate a mobile robot. The aim of the project was to move away from conventional controller principles and develop a more intuitive way of interaction.
The robot can move forwards, backwards, sideways, and rotate. It also features a gripper arm that can open, close, lift, and lower. These different functions had to be controlled through the controller in a natural and easy-to-understand way.

PROCESS

01 Concept & Prototyping

We began with sketches and discussions to explore different interaction ideas. The final concept became a squeezable ball that controls movement through tilting and the gripper through pressure.

02 User Testing

We conducted user testing to evaluate the intuitiveness and ease of use of our controller. Feedback from these sessions helped us refine the design and improve the overall user experience.

03 Implementation

We then built the final prototype, including 3D printing, sensor integration, and programming. Finally, we created renderings to show what the finished product could look like.
